# Custom deck template for Founder Identity Game
# Save this file and upload it from the app to use a tailored set of cards.
# Sections are optional; leave a section empty if you only want to override one part.

identityPillars:
# Format: title | short description | prompt | anchor1, anchor2, anchor3
Cross-disciplinary Builder | Science, AI, and implementation belong in the same conversation. | Show how your range helps you connect research, technology, and real-world execution. | Science, AI, Implementation
Practical Innovation | Ideas matter most when they become usable systems. | Explain how you move from possibility to something people can actually use. | Prototype, Workflow, Adoption
Societal Impact | Technology should create value beyond technical novelty. | Make the real-world relevance concrete, human, and believable. | Relevance, Access, Outcomes
Empowerment | Strong systems create opportunities for other people. | Frame your work as a way to unlock agency, capability, or participation. | Opportunity, Confidence, Capability
Curiosity & Adaptability | Continuous learning is part of the operating system. | Turn your self-directed path into evidence of resilience and growth. | Learning, Iteration, Range

challengeCards:
Explain your mission
Explain your leadership style to a future employee
Explain your value to a biotech partner
Explain why you are different from a pure researcher
Explain your vision without sounding unrealistic
Explain your biggest leadership risk
Explain why your self-taught path is a strength
Explain what societal impact means concretely
Explain what makes your founder story memorable
Explain why now is the right moment

audiences:
Investor | scalability, timing, and narrative conviction
Scientist | credibility, rigor, and intellectual honesty
Employee | leadership, culture, and trust
Corporate Partner | execution, reliability, and fit
NGO / Global Health Org | societal value, access, and grounded impact
Technical Cofounder | depth, judgment, and product direction
Customer | usefulness, clarity, and outcomes

constraints:
Explain without using the word AI
Explain in one sentence
Use a real-world example
Speak as if you are on stage
Speak casually over coffee
Use no technical jargon
Start with a concrete problem
End with why it matters
